SpendNote started as a personal finance app made for personal use first. I am sharing it in case anyone else wants to try a local-first way to track money without sending finance data to a central server.

What I like in this version:
- Track income, expenses, savings, budgets, reminders, and portfolio value in one place.
- Tap summary cards to see detailed entries for that week/month/period.
- Mark expenses that may be reducible, then include that total in reports.
- Add recurring or due items with one tick instead of typing the same thing again.
- Receipt scanning is included as a helper, but OCR is not always perfect, so everything can be reviewed before saving.
- Multi-currency support, up to 13 enabled currencies.
- 13 app languages are included. Some translations are AI-assisted, so please treat them as convenience text, not official localization.
- Portfolio tracking is manual by design. It is less automatic, but keeps the app private and avoids linking investment accounts.

How to try it:
- iPhone/iPad: open the PWA in Safari, tap Share, then Add to Home Screen.
- Android: install the APK from `releases/SpendNote-android-release.apk`. You may need to allow installing from your browser or file manager.
- Computer/browser: open the web app in a browser.
- PWA/web link: https://wpmoonsap-personal.github.io/SpendNote_PWA/

Offline/online:
- After the first load, basic recording can work offline from the installed PWA/app cache.
- Internet is still needed for first install/update, exchange-rate fetching, cloud/share actions, and anything that depends on another app or service.

Made mainly for personal use. Shared gently, not as financial advice.
